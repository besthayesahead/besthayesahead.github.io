Carantine

car•an•tine | kärənˌtēn |
(noun) a state or place of isolation (typically by motorized vehicle) in which sleep-deprived parents have put themselves in order to restore sanity by choosing joy
(verb) drive around for a period of time and act silly in order to restore sanity by choosing joy

When the world goes crazy, what can you do other than laugh? Joy is good medicine! Carantines started waaayy back in 2020, just after… the thing. And while most folks are no longer afraid to go to the store, we can always use a dose of joy! What will we do next?
